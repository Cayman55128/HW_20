Scope

The scope of this case study includes descriptions of all the UX/UI phases and tasks used to redefine the TCRP web site.

Target Audience

The target audience for the TCRP web site redesign are the following:

  • Family members or concerned advocates of someone afflicted with addiction.
  • Someone looking to donate to TCRP.

TCRP UX/UI Redesign Phases Overview

Research

In the research phase of the Twin Cities Recovery Project website redesign, the project team gathered information about the target audience, their needs, and the goals of the project. They conducted interviews, surveys, and usability testing to gain insights into user behavior and preferences. Based on their findings, they were able to define the project scope and goals and ideate potential design solutions that addressed the identified problems and aligned with the organization's mission and brand.

Learn More
Problem Statement

The Twin Cities Recovery Project website was outdated and difficult to navigate, which made it hard for people seeking addiction recovery resources in the Minneapolis-St. Paul area to find the information they needed. The organization sought to revamp the site to make it more user-friendly and to better serve their community.

Learn More
Definition and Ideation

During the definition and ideation phase of the Twin Cities Recovery website redesign, the team conducted a comprehensive analysis of the existing website to identify its strengths and weaknesses. Based on this analysis, the team developed a clear set of objectives and a detailed plan for the redesign, which included user research, content strategy, and design exploration.

Learn More
Prototyping Phase

During the prototyping phase of the Twin Cities Recovery website redesign, wireframes were created to establish the site's layout and structure, and high-fidelity mockups were designed to demonstrate the site's visual and interactive elements. These prototypes were tested with users to gather feedback and refine the design.

Learn More
Prototype Testing

During the prototype testing phase of the Twin Cities Recovery Project website redesign, a group of testers evaluated the website's functionality, usability, and overall user experience. The feedback received was then used to improve the website's design and ensure a positive user experience for all visitors.
